Jambeera Pinda Sweda

Jambeera Pinda Sweda, also known as Naranga (Lemon) Kizhi in Malayalam, is a specialized treatment performed by Kerala Vaidyas (Doctors) for treating conditions like cervical spondylosis and frozen joints. It involves a hot bolus fomentation (Pinda Sweda) using a mixture of Jambeera (Lemon) and herbal powders. This warm bolus, heated with or without medicated oils depending on the patient’s needs, is applied to muscles and joints too:

  • Relieve pain and inflammation
  • Alleviate tension
  • Improve flexibility
  • Rejuvenate and strengthen joints and soft tissues

Dhanyamla Dhara

Dhanyamla Dhara is a unique Ayurvedic therapy where warm herbal liquid is poured from a hung vessel over affected body parts. The therapy involves a preparation of cereals (Dhanya) and vinegar (Amla) obtained from citrus fruits, including horse gram, sliced fruits, Navara rice, and millets. These are immersed in water, boiled to extract therapeutic properties, forming a vinegar-like solution. The treatment typically lasts for about 45 to 60 minutes. Benefits:

  • Soothe the nervous system
  • Relieves physical tension
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Improves blood circulation
  • Revitalizes the body
  • Reduces pain (e.g., rheumatoid arthritis)
  • Balancing Pitta and Vata Doshas

Navarakizhi

Navarakizhi is a unique and deeply beneficial treatment using medicated herbal rice to nourish and strengthen the body. Specially cooked Navara rice blended with milk and herbs then slowly cook. The resulting fragrant mixture is contained in small linen bags or potlis. Navarakizhi is a two-step process. First, specially selected oils are applied all over the body. After this, the rice bags are massaged into the body, with extra focusing on nerve points (Marma).

  • Benefits includes rejuvenation, nourishment and strengthening against stresses and strains
  • Effective against rheumatism, joint and muscle pains, back pains (e.g., sciatica, slipped discs), high blood pressure and high cholesterol levels
  • Strengthens the nervous system, nourishes the tissues and enhances skin appearance
  • Mainly used for neurological disorders, paralysis, chronic rheumatism, arthritis, malnutrition of limbs, and recovery from fractures or stroke

Aschyotana & Anjana

Aschyotana and Anjana are specialized eye treatments that focus on improving eye health, treating eye conditions, and enhancing vision. Both therapies involve the application of medicinal substances to the eyes but differ in their techniques and purposes.

  • Aschyotana: An Ayurvedic technique for administering medicated eye drops. Derived from "Aschyota," meaning "to pour," it involves placing therapeutic drops directly into the eyes to treat various conditions.
  • Anjana: An Ayurvedic treatment where medicinal substances are applied to the conjunctiva (the membrane of the eye) to treat eye conditions. Derived from "Anjana," meaning "to apply," it focuses on improving eye health and alleviating discomfort.
