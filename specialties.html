Ayurvedic Consultations

At our center, Ayurvedic consultations offer personalized treatment plans based on the ancient Indian holistic healing system. Our Ayurvedic practitioners conduct a thorough assessment of individuals' physical, mental, and emotional states using techniques such as pulse diagnosis, tongue examination, and analysis of lifestyle factors. This comprehensive evaluation, forms the basis for tailored treatment plans.

These plans typically include dietary adjustments, herbal supplements, lifestyle modifications, and therapeutic practices such as yoga and meditation. The aim is to restore balance and harmony within the body by addressing the root cause of ailments rather than merely alleviating symptoms.

Ayurvedic consultations emphasize the interconnectedness of mind, body and spirit, offering holistic healing approaches that promote overall well-being and longevity.

Panchakarma Therapy

Panchakarma therapy stands as a cornerstone of Ayurvedic medicine, offering a profound detoxification and rejuvenation process that targets imbalances within the body's doshas, or bioenergetic forces. This comprehensive treatment comprises five main procedures, each meticulously designed to eliminate toxins, restore equilibrium, and promote overall wellness.

   Vamana: Involves therapeutic vomiting to expel excess Kapha dosha from the upper respiratory tract and stomach, it is beneficial for conditions such as asthma and allergies

   Virechana: Utilizing herbal laxatives, Virechana induces controlled purgation to cleanse the gastrointestinal tract and blood of accumulated Pitta dosha. It is effective for liver disorders and skin ailments.

   Basti: Considered a cornerstone therapy, Basti administers medicated enemas to purge accumulated Vata dosha from the colon. It provides relief from issues such as constipation and arthritis.

   Nasya: Involves nasal administration of medicated oils to clear congestion and enhance sensory functions.

  Raktamokshana: Though less common today, Raktamokshana addresses impurities in the blood through therapeutic bloodletting.

Panchakarma therapy is conducted under expert guidance and involves preparatory treatments before and a post-treatment regimen after. This ensures sustained benefits and holistic well-being by addressing the root causes of ailments and promoting overall health.

SMRITHI MEDITATION

Smriti Meditation, developed by Professor (Dr) K V Dilip Kumar, is a distinctive guided interactive meditation technique rooted in Ayurveda. Unlike many popular forms of meditation that primarily aim for deep relaxation, Smriti Meditation serves a therapeutic purpose within Ayurvedic principles.

In Ayurveda, the concept of Prajnaparadha (Intellectual error) is recognized as a significant contributor to the manifestation of psychic and psychosomatic diseases. According to texts like the 'Charaka Samhita', Prajnaparadha disrupts mental equilibrium and can lead to various ailments. Ayurvedic treatment, therefore, includes Satvavajaya (psychotherapy), which incorporates various tools including Smriti Meditation.

The components of Satvavajaya chikitsa, as outlined in Ayurveda, include:

  • Jnana (Knowledge)
  • Vijnana (Specific Knowledge)
  • Dhairya (Courage of sensory control)
  • Smriti (Memory)
  • Samadhi (Meditation)

Smriti Meditation specifically emphasizes the role of memory (Smriti) and meditation (Samadhi) as therapeutic tools. It is designed to guide individuals through a structured meditation practice that not only promotes relaxation but also aims to address underlying psychological and emotional issues. This approach is conducted under the guidance of a specialized Ayurvedic doctor or practitioner who understands the nuances of Ayurvedic psychotherapy.

In contrast to some yogic meditation practices that focus predominantly on spiritual development, Smriti Meditation aligns closely with Ayurvedic principles by integrating psychological healing with meditative techniques. Its goal is to facilitate a holistic approach to wellness, aiming to alleviate psychic and psychosomatic ailments through structured meditation sessions tailored to individual needs.
